🚴 UCI Road Cycling Blog — April & May 2026

April 1–6 — Flemish Classics Explosion

Recap

April opened with Dwars door Vlaanderen, where Filippo Ganna caught Wout van Aert in the final meters of a dramatic 184.6 km race. Days later, Tadej Pogačar won the Tour of Flanders (278 km), defeating van der Poel and Evenepoel with the full elite field present.

Meanwhile, the Basque Country began with Paul Seixas winning the opening time trial and taking the race lead immediately.

Rider Analysis

Ganna proved he can compete in elite Classics beyond time trials, while Pogačar validated himself against every major cobbled contender. Seixas shifted from promising rider to stage-race controller.

Prediction

Attention turned to whether Pogačar could handle Roubaix’s brutality and if Seixas could convert his early lead into a major GC statement.

April 7–13 — Roubaix & Seixas Breakthrough

Recap

Paul Seixas dominated Itzulia Basque Country, winning three stages and the GC by over 2 minutes — becoming the youngest winner ever. Paris–Roubaix followed, where Wout van Aert won the fastest edition ever, beating Pogačar in a sprint.

Rider Analysis

Van Aert’s Roubaix win marked redemption and national significance. Seixas’ control of Itzulia represented one of the biggest breakthroughs of the spring. Pogačar enhanced his reputation with Flanders + Roubaix results in one week.

Prediction

The Ardennes would reward climbers, with Evenepoel, Pogačar, and Seixas expected to emerge as the key riders.

April 14–19 — Evenepoel Wins Amstel

Recap

Remco Evenepoel won Amstel Gold Race after a long breakaway with Skjelmose, finishing nearly two minutes ahead of the chasing group.

Rider Analysis

Evenepoel asserted himself as a top Ardennes rider, while Skjelmose confirmed elite status. The race gap highlighted how selective Amstel became.

Prediction

La Flèche Wallonne would favour explosive climbers, especially riders arriving with strong climbing rhythm like Seixas.

April 20–26 — Ardennes Finale

Recap

Seixas won Flèche Wallonne, becoming the youngest ever winner, while Pogačar claimed Liège–Bastogne–Liège after dropping him late.

Rider Analysis

Seixas confirmed himself as a major player, while Pogačar remained the benchmark, securing his fourth Liège win.

Prediction

Focus moved to Romandie, testing if Classics form could transition into stage-race dominance.

April 27–May 3 — Romandie Dominance

Recap

Pogačar dominated Tour de Romandie, winning 4 stages and GC. Georg Zimmermann also claimed a major win at Eschborn–Frankfurt.

Rider Analysis

Pogačar operated at a different level, while Lipowitz and Martinez showed strong GC potential. Zimmermann achieved a career-defining victory.

Prediction

The Giro would shift the sport toward endurance and long-term consistency.

May 4–10 — Giro Begins

Recap

The Giro opened in Bulgaria with sprint stages won by Paul Magnier and Guillermo Silva.

Rider Analysis

The early race favored sprinters and opportunists, with GC contenders staying cautious.

Prediction

Mountain stages would soon define the race hierarchy.

May 11–17 — Giro Takes Shape

Recap

Multiple winners emerged, while Afonso Eulálio held the lead. Vingegaard won key mountain stages.

Rider Analysis

Eulálio became a surprise leader, while Vingegaard emerged as the strongest climber.

Prediction

Time trial and mid-race stages would determine if the lead could hold.

May 18–24 — Vingegaard Takes Control

Recap

Vingegaard took the overall lead on Stage 14 and began extending his advantage.

Rider Analysis

This week defined the race hierarchy, with Vingegaard becoming dominant while stage wins remained diverse.

Prediction

Final week would determine margins rather than the winner.

May 25–31 — Giro Conclusion

Recap

Jonas Vingegaard won the Giro, with multiple stage wins and dominance in the mountains.

Rider Analysis

This performance cemented Vingegaard’s place as a Grand Tour leader. Gall and Hindley completed a strong podium, while Magnier and Ciccone won secondary classifications.

Prediction

The season now turns toward the Tour de France, with rivalry between Pogačar and Vingegaard intensifying.
