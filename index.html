Week of April 5, 2026

Monument Glory at the Tour of Flanders

Pogačar conquers Flanders, Ganna sets the tone in Belgium, and Paul Seixas launches a breakthrough run at Itzulia Basque Country.

+
Tour of Flanders • April 5, 2026 Dwars door Vlaanderen • April 1, 2026 Itzulia Basque Country • April 6–11, 2026

Recap

The first major highlight of April came with the Tour of Flanders (Ronde van Vlaanderen) on April 5, 2026, where Tadej Pogačar delivered a commanding performance to take victory in one of cycling’s most iconic cobbled Monuments. This win capped off an intense early Classics campaign and reaffirmed his dominance across multiple terrain types.

Earlier in the week, Dwars door Vlaanderen (April 1) saw Filippo Ganna claim victory, setting the tone for a highly competitive Belgian classics block. Meanwhile, the stage race Itzulia Basque Country (April 6–11) began immediately after Flanders, with emerging French rider Paul Seixas later securing the overall win, marking the start of a breakthrough period for him.

Taken together, this week represented the transition from early-season form testing to decisive Monument racing, where contenders began to reveal their true ambitions for the spring.

Rider Analysis

Tadej Pogačar once again stood out as the defining rider of the moment. His victory at Flanders—a race traditionally dominated by specialists in cobbles and positioning—demonstrates not only his physical strength but also his tactical maturity in chaotic one-day racing. Winning both mountainous races earlier in the season and now a cobbled Monument highlights his rare all-round capability and growing status as the peloton’s most complete rider.

Equally compelling is the rise of Paul Seixas, whose overall win at Itzulia Basque Country signals a major step forward in his development. Already competitive in stage races, Seixas is beginning to show consistency against elite competition, suggesting he could become a serious GC contender in upcoming Ardennes races and beyond. His trajectory contrasts with that of established stars, showcasing the ongoing generational shift within the sport.

Meanwhile, riders such as Wout van Aert and Mathieu van der Poel remained highly visible but were forced into supporting roles or podium contention rather than outright dominance during this particular week. This subtle shift hints at a more open—and unpredictable—spring Classics landscape.

Prediction

Looking ahead, the immediate focus shifts to Paris–Roubaix (April 12), where cobbled specialists are expected to reassert themselves. Despite Pogačar’s Flanders win, Roubaix’s unique brutality may favor riders like Wout van Aert, who ultimately capitalized on these conditions later in April to claim victory. However, Pogačar’s current form makes him impossible to discount, and a strong showing would further underline his versatility.

Beyond Roubaix, attention will turn to the Ardennes Classics, where climbing specialists typically dominate. Here, riders like Remco Evenepoel and Seixas are likely to come to the forefront, particularly on steeper finishes such as La Flèche Wallonne and Liège–Bastogne–Liège. Given recent results, expect an intense rivalry between Pogačar and the emerging challengers, especially as fatigue from the cobbled races begins to influence outcomes.

Overall, the trajectory suggests a spring defined by both dominance at the top and a growing depth of contenders capable of challenging in different race profiles.
