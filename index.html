🚴 UCI Road Cycling Weekly Blog

Week of April 12, 2026 – Paris–Roubaix

Recap

The second week of April was dominated by the legendary Paris–Roubaix (April 12, 2026), where Wout van Aert triumphed in a dramatic sprint finish ahead of Tadej Pogačar. Known as the “Hell of the North,” Roubaix delivered its usual chaos, with cobbled sectors splintering the field and rewarding both resilience and tactical discipline. Concurrently, the Itzulia Basque Country reached its conclusion, with Paul Seixas securing the overall victory after a consistent and aggressive week in the mountains.

Together, these races marked the clear transition from cobbled power racing into terrain favoring climbers, effectively closing the Northern Classics phase and ushering in the Ardennes.

Rider Analysis

Van Aert’s victory represents resilience after years of near-misses. His ability to outsprint Pogačar demonstrates his status as a premier Classics rider. Meanwhile, Seixas showed maturity across a full stage race, signaling a generational shift. Pogačar further enhanced his reputation as the most versatile rider in the peloton.

Prediction

Focus shifts to the Ardennes Classics. Climbers like Evenepoel, Pogačar, and Seixas are expected to dominate, with fatigue playing a major role.

Week of April 19, 2026 – Amstel Gold Race

Recap

The Ardennes began with Remco Evenepoel winning Amstel Gold Race (April 19), a tactically controlled race defined by climbing and timing.

The peloton transitioned fully into terrain requiring sustained power and positioning.

Rider Analysis

Evenepoel showed tactical growth. Van Aert and Van der Poel struggled to adapt, while Pogačar remained consistent. Adaptability is now key.

Prediction

The Mur de Huy will produce explosive racing favoring climbers like Evenepoel and Seixas.

Week of April 26, 2026 – Ardennes Finale

Recap

Seixas won La Flèche Wallonne and Pogačar claimed Liège–Bastogne–Liège.

These results highlighted contrasting strengths: explosive climbing vs endurance.

Rider Analysis

Seixas confirmed elite climbing status, while Pogačar remained the benchmark across all terrain types.

Prediction

Transition shifts toward stage racing and Grand Tours. Fatigue may influence results.

Week of May 3, 2026 – Tour de Romandie

Recap

Pogačar secured overall victory at the Tour de Romandie, showcasing control across climbing and time trials.

Rider Analysis

His dominance reflects total stage-race mastery, outperforming specialists lacking consistency.

Prediction

The Giro will test endurance rather than versatility, with Vingegaard emerging as a key challenger.

Recap

Jonas Vingegaard secured overall victory with dominant mountain performances.

Rider Analysis

His consistency and endurance underline Grand Tour mastery, contrasting with Pogačar’s spring dominance.

Prediction
